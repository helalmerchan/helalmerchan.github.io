Chapter 1: HIV and AIDS

1. Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) targets and impairs the immune system.

2. Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome (AIDS) is the first phase of HIV infection.

3. Unprotected sexual intercourse NEVER results in transmission of HIV.

4. Sharing used syringe needles IS a common mode of transmission of HIV.

5. "Standard precautions" are a series of infection control practices to reduce or prevent the transmission of bloodborne pathogens or other infectious conditions.

6. "Universal precautions" means assuming that bodily fluids are ALWAYS safe and free from any bloodborne pathogens.

7. Antiretroviral therapy (ART) may prevent the HIV virus from multiplying, reducing the viral load of the infection.

8. Consistently using condoms every time you engage in sexual intercourse will help to prevent HIV transmission.

10. Treat all persons with HIV/AIDS with respect, compassion and understanding.

11. If an HIV test result is positive, the healthcare provider must notify only the patient, NOT the county health department.

12. Under Florida law, those who test positive for HIV or other STDs have NO legal obligation to tell sexual partners of their infectious status.
